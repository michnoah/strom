Data Requests

Request a Copy of Your Data

You can request a copy of the personal data associated with your account by starting a conversation via email. This page explains what to include so we can verify your identity and respond efficiently.

strombearer@gmail.com

What to include in your email

  • Your full name and the email address used for your account.
  • Your phone number.
  • Your Google Play account email.
  • Your region/country of residence.
  • The type of request: data export, data deletion, or both.
  • Any date ranges or specific datasets you want (optional).

Identity verification

To protect your privacy, we may ask you to re-verify your email address and provide limited additional information that helps us confirm ownership of the account.

  • We will never ask for your password or full payment card details.
  • If you’re writing from a different email, we may ask you to confirm from the (GooglePlay) account email.

Timeline

  • We acknowledge your request by email and start verification.
  • Once verified, we begin processing. If your request is complex, we’ll keep you updated.
  • We deliver your data securely via a downloadable archive or share confirmation of deletion, as applicable.

Format of your data

Exports are provided in commonly used, machine‑readable formats whenever possible (for example, JSON or CSV in a zipped archive).

Email template (copy & paste)

To: strombearer@gmail.com
Subject: Data Request - [Your Name]

Hello Strom Team,

I'd like to request a copy of my data.

Name: [Your full name]
Primary email on account: [your@email.example]
Region/country of residence: [Your country]

I am requesting: [Export of my data | Deletion of my account data | Both]

Optional details:
- Date range for data: [from] to [to]
- Specific datasets of interest: [e.g., activity logs, profile, settings]

Thank you,
[Your Name]

Questions we can help with

  • What personal data we hold about your account.
  • How to request corrections or updates.
  • How deletion works and what data we must retain (e.g., for fraud prevention or legal obligations).
